\section{Introduction}

\newacronym{sm}{SM}{Standard Model}
\newacronym{lhc}{LHC}{Large Hadron Collider}
\newacronym{lum}{$\mathcal{L}$}{luminosity}

Understanding the universe at its most fundamental level is the driving force behind particle physics research. Central to this research
are particle colliders, machines that accelerate and collide particles, revealing new insights through the analysis of the byproducts of
such collisions. The performance of these colliders largely hinges on a critical factor: the \acrfull{lum}. This parameter,
which measures the rate at which particles pass through a given area, directly influences the number of collisions a collider can produce
and stands as a proportionality factor between the number of collisions and the cross section of the process under study. This relation with
the cross section is particularly important, and gives paramount importance to the precise determination of luminosity.
