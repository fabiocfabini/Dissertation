The activities begin with an extensive literature review to establish a working understanding of the important role of luminosity to particle physics research as well as how it is measured and how it impacts the experiments at the LHC. This is followed by the consistent development and maintenance of the framework responsible for providing both online luminosity measurements and offline analysis of the data collected from all kinds of scans. Any analysis tool or framework that needs development or improvment will be done in parallel with the analysis of the data collected from the scans. The analysis will concern data collected from the 2023 data taking period. On par with the analysis, the development and improvement of the fit functions will be done to ensure the accuracy of the results. The final corrections and uncertainties will be taken into consideration to ensure the validity and reliability of the research findings. Finally, the last few months will include the thesis writing.

As this is a research project, the activities are subject to change depending on the results obtained and the needs of the project. Any changes will be indentified and discussed with the supervisor and the co-supervisor.
