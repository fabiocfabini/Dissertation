\chapter*{Abstract}

In the realm of high-energy physics experiments, the precise measurement of luminosity, a pivotal parameter closely linked to the quantity of acquired data, assumes paramount significance.
This measurement holds an important role in ascertaining the absolute cross section of physical processes and in the pursuit of searching for new physics,
and is thus used by nearly all physics analyses conducted within experiments.

This drives dedicated efforts at the Compact Muon Solenoid (CMS) collaboration at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) of the European Organization for Nuclear Research (CERN).
State-of-the-art investigations necessitate the analysis of data gathered through specialized detectors known as luminometers, 
via a procedure called van der Meer (vdM) scan, which determines data calibration for luminosity assessment.
Additionally, it involves the utilization of a sophisticated software framework, the vdM framework.

This project serves a dual purpose within this context. Firstly, it aims to optimize the vdM framework, increasing its speed, user-friendliness, and functionality.
Such improvements are indispensable to meet the stringent deadlines for delivering luminosity measurement results, aligning with the time needs of the CMS experiment.

Concurrently, this project contributes to the analysis of collision data
acquired by the luminometers through the application of a series of detailed
corrections during the offline analysis process to effectively mitigate systematic effects and minimize the overall uncertainty in order to enhance the
precision of the luminosity measurement.
